Auto Insurance Medical Options


On July 1st, Michigan No-Fault insurance is changing!

Let’s review what we’ve discussed in previous newsletters: 

Minimum liability limits are moving from 20/40 up to a default of 250/500

You can choose a lower liability limit (as low as 50/100) by signing a form at every renewal, but after these changes, there will be more potential for lawsuits, so make sure your liability limits are sufficient

You have the choice to opt-in or opt-out of unlimited lifetime medical (PIP) coverage (opting-out has several requirements)

If you opt-in for unlimited lifetime medical coverage, the MCCA fee will drop to $100 per year, if you opt-out (and follow additional requirements) then you won’t pay anything into the MCCA.

A driver at a different address, or someone living with you who isn’t a relative, should have their own auto insurance policy so that they are completely covered

The main part of No-Fault insurance that is changing is the Personal Injury Protection (PIP) coverages. There will be a total of 6 different PIP options, so let’s review them! 

Option 1 - Unlimited: You get unlimited lifetime medical coverage. This is what you currently have through No-Fault, and you currently pay an MCCA fee of $220 per year, per vehicle, it will change to only $100 per year, per vehicle. We recommend this option because it provides the most expansive coverage. 

With each of the following options, there is no MCCA fee:

Option 2 - $500,000: Your medical coverage is limited to $500,000.

Option 3 - $250,000: Your medical coverage is limited to $250,000

Option 4* - $250,000 excluding all or some person(s) from PIP Medical: Your medical coverage is limited to $250,000, and some household family members can be excluded if they have Qualified Health Coverage.*

Option 5* - $50,000 (Medicaid option): Your medical coverage is limited to $50,000. You must be enrolled in Medicaid, and other family members must have Qualified Health Coverage.

Option 6* - Medicare Opt-Out: You must be enrolled in Medicare Parts A & B, and all other household family members must have Qualified Health Coverage. In this case, Medicare Parts A & B will cover medical claims from an auto accident, so there would be no medical coverage from your auto policy, just from Medicare.

*Qualified Health Coverage is healthcare that covers injuries from auto accidents. 

While these options allow you to lower your premium, remember that if you choose a limit on your medical coverage, you would be personally responsible for costs above your chosen limit. Be sure that you’re covered!  

In the next few months, you’ll get a form in the mail from your auto insurance company requesting that you choose a PIP coverage option. Before signing these forms, make sure you know what you’re choosing!

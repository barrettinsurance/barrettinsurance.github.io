So will I get cheaper rates? Not so fast. . .

Green light: The $220 per year MCCA (Michigan Catastrophic Claims Association) fee will be reduced or completely disappear, depending on how much medical coverage you choose. 

Red light: The state minimum liability limits will be raised to a split limit of 250/500, so that has the potential to balance out the lower MCCA fee in your rate. 

Yes, some of the charges from No-Fault coverage will disappear, but there are still parts of the new law being written and we don’t know exactly what will happen to rates until we get closer to the changes. 

There are many other changes involved with the upcoming legislation - we will continue to keep you updated on the changes in the following newsletters!

Would You Buy an Electric Truck?

Tesla just unveiled their electric truck to be released in 2021, and Ford has hinted at an electric F-150 to be released in the next five years.

The Tesla truck will have a payload capacity of 3,500 pounds, and should be able to tow 14,000 pounds. The Tesla truck has a 17 inch touch screen, and it will have all the fancy features - except an automatic coffee maker.

How would an electric car affect my insurance rate?

Insurance rates on electric vehicles are generally more expensive due to unique repair costs and electric cars being more expensive in the first place, but as they become more popular and accepted, insurance rates for electric cars are nearly identical to rates for fuel powered cars.

If you decide to take the electric car route, another thing to remember is to add coverage for your charging station on to your homeowners policy.

Oh, and don’t forget to plug in the car at night!

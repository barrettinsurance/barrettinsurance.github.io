You’ve Arrived at Your Destination...


The new Michigan auto insurance laws have been around for an entire month now!

What’s up with my rate? Some of you have seen your rate go down and that’s great, while some of you have unfortunately seen it jump up. While the state fees and medical rates were reduced, some discounts like having good credit or owning a home were taken away from your policy.

Where are my forms? If you haven’t gotten your forms in the mail yet, that’s okay! Most companies will send them 30-45 days before your renewal date, so you may not see the paperwork until then. As always, if you have any questions when you get your forms in the mail, give us a call or schedule an appointment at 517-849-1000!

Medicare Opt-out

If you’re on Medicare parts A & B, you now have the option to completely opt out of medical coverage from your auto policy. However, there are two things to consider with this option:

    1. Medicare will only pay for 80% of your out-patient hospital visit, or you may have a large deductible for an in-patient stay, so we recommend you have a Medicare Supplement or an Advantage Plan to cover those other costs

    2. Medicare does not cover any attendant care costs (the unlimited coverage plan does)

What does attendant care cover? I’m glad you asked!

    • In-home health services

    • Long-term comprehensive rehabilitation

    • Electric wheelchairs, walkers, or other equipment

    • Physical, occupational, massage, or brain function therapy

    • Assistive devices such as electronics or fitness equipment

    • Transportation to/from medical appointments

    • Vehicle or house modifications for accessibility

    • Replacement services for household duties

    • Case management services

    • Wage loss if you’re still working

These attendant care services are covered under the unlimited lifetime medical option, but are excluded under the medicare option. Make sure you know what you’re choosing! If you have questions about any of the new auto insurance options then give us a call! 517-849-1000

Let's Play Ball


America’s pastime has started back up! It’s not exactly normal, but playing baseball is about as close as you can get.

After conflict between players associations and team owners, as well as figuring out how to play while keeping everyone healthy, it was agreed to have a 60 game regular season and a bigger-than-normal playoffs.

Here’s the catch: There are no fans, no spitting of sunflower seeds or tobacco, and no celebratory chest bumps. Also, unrelated to the Coronavirus, the National League now allows for a designated hitter and a pitcher must face a minimum of 3-batters (no more pitching changes for 1 at-bat)

BLT

If you look at the Webster's dictionary definition of “summer,” you’ll see that bacon, lettuce, and tomato are essential. Well, at least those definitions ought to be there.

To start your fancy BLT, you'll need thick-cut bacon, some heavy bread worthy of good memories - like sourdough, and heirloom tomatoes are pretty good as well!

And of course, a good BLT has a lot more than B, L, and T: 

    • Add some health and taste with avocados or guacamole

    • Make it on the griddle, or actually grill it

    • Add a runny, bright-yolked egg

    • Add your favorite tomato pesto

    • Nacho cheese is always nice

    • Salt and pepper or other spices

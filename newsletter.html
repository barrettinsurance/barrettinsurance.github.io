So what should you do when you start sliding?

Steer into, not against, the skid and let off the throttle to get the car back under control. This "don't fight it" approach should be effective with front-wheel drive and all-wheel-drive vehicles.

If you have rear-wheel drive, you may need to counter-steer to avoid having the back end of the car slide, but this is hard to get right. Generally speaking, rear-wheel-drive cars and snow or ice don't get along well, so it's best to keep them in the garage — especially anything with gobs of horsepower.

And of course, the best way to avoid skidding is to drive very slowly in the first place. Increase your braking distance and give yourself more travel time when it snows!

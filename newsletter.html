The Odds of Being Confused by the New Michigan Auto Insurance Laws?

Pretty high. . . But that’s okay, we’re here to help you understand the changes!

Are all your ducks in a row? 

Let’s review what we’ve discussed in previous newsletters: 

 • Minimum liability limits are moving from 20/40 up to 250/500

 • You have the choice to opt in our opt out of unlimited lifetime medical (PIP) coverage (opting out has several requirements)

 • If you opt in for unlimited lifetime medical coverage, the MCCA fee will drop to $100 per year, if you opt out (and follow additional requirements) then you won’t pay anything into the MCCA.

This month, we’re going to discuss what is called the “order of priority,” which organizes the people that are covered by a medical claim. If drivers on your policy are at a separate address, or if you have a non-relative living at your address, then the order of priority will exclude those people from unlimited lifetime medical coverage. 

Here are the new regulations: 

Currently: 

On July 1st, 2019, legislation went into effect so that any driver who lives outside of the named insured’s household is subject to a $250,000 limit of medical coverage. These drivers would need to be covered by their own policy.

What if my child (or anyone listed on my policy) is living at a different location?  

Under current law, this driver would not be eligible for unlimited medical coverage (they would be under a limit of $250,000).

What if I have a roommate or friend living with me?

Even if they are listed on your policy, non-relatives who live at your address would need to have their own insurance policy to qualify for unlimited medical coverage. 

After July 1st, 2020:

What if my child is living at a different location? Any driver at a different address would not be eligible to be listed on your policy. They would need their own policy, even if they’re driving your car. 

What if I have a roommate or friend living with me? If they’re not relatives and they live at your address, they would need to have their own policy - even if they’re listed on your policy. 

Bottom line - a driver at a different address, or someone living with you who isn’t a relative, should have their own auto insurance policy so that they are completely covered. 

What if my child is away at college? The good news is that nothing will change in this situation, they are covered both now and after July 1st!

Got Garlic?


Garlic Parmesan Dinner Rolls

Ingredients: 

 • 2 packets active dry yeast, (total 1/2 oz or 14g + 1 tablespoon sugar)

 • 1/4 cup warm water

 • 1 1/4 cups milk

 • 5 tablespoons sugar

 • 3/4 teaspoon salt

 • 1/4 cup unsalted butter (1/2 stick butter)

 • 4 1/2 cups sifted all-purpose flour

 • 1 large egg

Toppings: 

 • 4 cloves garlic, finely minced

 • 1/2 cup salted butter, melted

 • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley

 • 1/3 cup shredded Parmesan cheese

Instructions: 

 • Dissolve the yeast plus 1 tablespoon of sugar in warm water. Then heat the milk with the sugar, salt, butter until lukewarm.

 • Add the egg to the yeast mixture. Combine the yeast mixture, the milk mixture, and all of the flour. Stir to combine well. Cover the dough and rest for 15 minutes. Using a stand mixer with a dough hook, knead the dough for 5 minutes.

 • Preheat oven to 350F.

 • On a floured surface, roll out the dough and and divide it into 24 balls. Roll out each ball and tie each into a knot. Cover and let rise for 25 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare the garlic-herb butter by combining all the Toppings ingredients together, except Parmesan cheese. Brush the butter mixture generously on the surface and top with Parmesan cheese; bake for 10 minutes. Brush with the remaining butter mixture and serve warm.

Liability Changes


Michigan auto insurance laws are changing July 1st!

Let’s review what we’ve discussed in previous newsletters: 

 • Minimum liability limits are moving from 20/40 up to 250/500

 • You have the choice to opt in our opt out of unlimited lifetime medical (PIP) coverage (opting out has several requirements)

 • If you opt in for unlimited lifetime medical coverage, the MCCA fee will drop to $100 per year, if you opt out (and follow additional requirements) then you won’t pay anything into the MCCA.

 • A driver at a different address, or someone living with you who isn’t a relative, should have their own auto insurance policy so that they are completely covered

Let’s dig into liability limits a little further! 

First, bodily injury liability is increasing from $20,000 per person and $40,000 per event (aka 20/40) all the way up to $250,000 per person and $500,000 per event (aka 250/500). Part of the reason that Michigan’s bodily injury liability limits were only at 20/40 is because everyone’s medical costs were covered by no-fault insurance (the MCCA). Now that there are options to opt out of unlimited lifetime medical coverage (more on that next month!), it opens up the ability of other drivers to sue for medical costs that aren’t covered by their insurance, so the liability limits were increased. 

Second, if you want to decrease your bodily injury liability limits, you would be required to sign a form every time your auto policy renews, and you can choose as low as a split limit of 50/100. However! After July 1st, there will be less restrictions on bodily injury lawsuits, and you would be responsible for any costs that go beyond your policy limits. Make sure your bodily injury liability limits are adequate, and you may want to consider raising your liability limits, depending on your situation and your assets!
